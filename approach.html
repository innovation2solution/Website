The i2s Methodology

Audit. Incubate. Upscale. — A 360° approach to digital transformation

Our methodology isn't a rigid process imposed on every engagement. It's a framework we adapt to your organisation — combining the discipline of PRINCE2, the agility of Scrum, and the service thinking of ITIL into one cohesive approach that delivers real outcomes.

The Three Pillars

Everything we do sits within three phases

Audit. Incubate. Upscale. Three words — but a comprehensive philosophy for how transformation should be approached.

01 — Audit

We start with an honest, objective assessment of where you are. Technology landscape, processes, data, people, culture, governance. No assumptions — just evidence. This is the foundation everything else is built on.

02 — Incubate

We design and develop solutions carefully, iteratively, and with continuous stakeholder involvement. Every sprint produces working, testable software or demonstrable progress. No big-bang reveals. No surprises at go-live.

03 — Upscale

Deployment is the beginning, not the end. We establish training, change management, and continuous improvement cycles that grow the value of your investment — adapting as your organisation and the technology landscape evolves.

In Practice

What every engagement looks like

01

Stakeholder Alignment & Discovery

Before any technical work begins, we invest in stakeholder alignment. We meet with sponsors, end users, IT teams, and business leaders to understand not just what's needed, but what success really looks like — and what the political and organisational dynamics are that will shape the programme.

02

Current State Assessment

We conduct a structured review of your existing technology, processes, data, and capabilities. This isn't a superficial audit — it goes deep enough to surface the real issues: the technical debt, the process inefficiencies, the compliance gaps, and the cultural blockers. The output is a clear, evidence-based picture of where you are.

03

Solution Design & Business Case

We design the future state and produce a clear business case with prioritised options, cost estimates, benefits profiles, and risk assessments. Stakeholders understand exactly what they're approving and what they're getting. We present options — we don't dictate a single solution.

04

Agile Delivery with Governance

We deliver in iterations — using Agile sprints where appropriate, or a structured Waterfall programme where regulatory, contractual, or organisational context demands it. Either way, there's a clear governance framework: regular steering updates, risk registers, issue logs, and transparent reporting throughout.

05

Training, Change & Communications

Change management isn't a workstream we bolt on at the end. It runs in parallel with delivery from day one — stakeholder communications plans, change impact assessments, training needs analysis, and user acceptance testing all planned and resourced as core programme activities.

06

Post-Launch Support & Continuous Improvement

After go-live, we run a structured post-implementation review, establish ongoing support arrangements, and set up continual service improvement cycles. We measure the benefits we promised, and we're accountable for delivering them.
